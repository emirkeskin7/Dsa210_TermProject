DSA 210 Project Proposal: Predicting Formula 1 Mechanical DNFsMotivationIn the high-stakes world of Formula 1, mechanical reliability is a critical strategic component. A car failing to finish a race (DNF) is often not merely a matter of bad luck, but a result of cumulative environmental stress, track characteristics, and component fatigue. The objective of this project is to investigate the predictability of mechanical failures by modeling their relationship with external factors such as track altitude and ambient temperature. This study aims to provide a data-driven perspective on the trade-off between peak performance and mechanical durability.Data Source & CollectionTo satisfy the project’s data enrichment requirement, two distinct data streams will be integrated:Race Data: Historical race results and mechanical status codes (2014–2025) will be retrieved using the Jolpica F1 API (the community-maintained successor to the Ergast API) and the FastF1 Python library.Enrichment (Environmental Data): This dataset will be enriched with historical weather and geographical data, specifically track altitude and ambient temperature, sourced via the Open-Meteo API.Collection Method: All data will be collected using Python-based scripts utilizing the requests and FastF1 libraries, and subsequently processed and merged using pandas.Data CharacteristicsThe final dataset will cover the "Hybrid Era" of Formula 1 (2014–2025), comprising approximately 4,500 individual driver-race entries.Features: Track altitude (meters), ambient temperature (°C), circuit type (street vs. permanent), power unit manufacturer, and driver experience (total career starts).Target Variable: Mechanical DNF status (Binary: 0 = Finished, 1 = Mechanical Failure).Planned Analysis & Machine LearningThe initial phase will involve Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Hypothesis Testing (T-Tests) to evaluate the impact of high altitude and extreme heat on failure rates. For the machine learning phase, I will implement a Random Forest Classifier or Logistic Regression model to predict the likelihood of a mechanical failure based on the identified stress factors. Model performance will be evaluated through Feature Importance analysis to determine whether environmental conditions or component fatigue are the primary drivers of unreliability.
